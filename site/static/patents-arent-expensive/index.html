Information

In part because of extremely successful lobbying by big Pharma, the US grants monopolies (patents) for almost anything, including changing the color of a pill — even if the company being granted the patent spent little money on the research or innovation of the treatment.

For example, drug corporation Gilead produces the life saving-drug Sovaldi, a Hepatitis C treatment with an unparalleled cure rate, at a cost of approximately $68 per 12-week treatment (which is the full course). They license it in India to be produced and sold at a price of between $400 and $900 per treatment, while in the U.S. it’s at a price of $84,000 per treatment. 1, 2, 3 The extreme difference in price is possible because although the treatment is extremely effective, it is a minor change to an old drug used for a different purpose (in treating HIV and AIDS), and India does not grant monopolies (patents) on the basis of minor tweaks — only scientific innovation.
